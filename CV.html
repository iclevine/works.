Project 1: Lasercutting
I started thinking I wanted to make a record shelf with a spectogram (frequency analysis) of a song etched into it
It would be an amalgamation of plywood and acrylic, with the spectrogram laser engraved into the acrylic part.

Spectrogram:
I first tested this by cutting and engraving a few parts at the laser cutter and more than liking the parts themselves, I noticed a cool effect on the edges.
So, in consultation with Professor Brueckner I decided to make my project more of an exploration of acrylic engraving and the virtues of that.
I already have a record shelf anyway.
The first thought I had about layered acrylic was about etching ascii art onto it for some reason.
In searching for ascii optical illusions I stumbled upon chris.com which inspired me with the stereograms page.
that page
I began my process by adapting some of their designs to the lasercutter, like the dot grid.
Here's my first cut files and the results.

These were pretty cool but didn't have that much of a dimensional effect,
in addition the diamond shape didn't cut out because it was formatted as text instead of as a vector in illustrator.
So I went back in illustrator and re-did the diamond with all lines and added a few other crazy shapes that were slated to do moire effects when layered.


Some of these came out cool and I fooled around with them and plan on fleshing them out further. The first one is the diamond

It has a nice 3D effect and the layers translated well.
I hope to make a bigger one with more layers of this one at some point.
The next one is the kinetic moire circles.
I made these by having the laser cutter etch circles very close to each other repeatedly.
it made this rough, and more organic texture.

I initially intended it to look like a rose when layered but more it looked like a pyramid cake or a pile of dirt
I didn't have a long enough 6-32 screw to put it all together as one composite shape but ended up doing it with spacers.
And then I noticed it could spin, occasionally lining up the pattern originally intended.
I plan on putting a servo motor behind it and making it a simple kinetic sculpture.
